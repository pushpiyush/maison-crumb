The person behind the oven

Hi, I'm the one with flour on her sleeves.

Maison Crumb started in a kitchen that was never meant to be a business — just a counter where I tested birthday cakes for friends because the grocery store version always tasted like nothing. A few orders turned into a waitlist, and the waitlist turned into this.

I still bake everything myself, in small batches, usually with something simmering on the stove and a notebook of flavor combinations I haven't tried yet. Nothing leaves the kitchen that I wouldn't serve at my own table — that's the only rule that's never changed.

These days most orders are custom: a flavor someone remembers from their grandmother's kitchen, a cake shaped like a stubborn family pet, a cookie box for a coworker who's having a hard week. If you can describe it, there's a good chance we can bake it.
